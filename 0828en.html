First Days in Italy

Last Saturday, I arrived at my accommodation at 9 in the evening. The bed was made and everything was prepared. My landlord asked if I wanted something light to eat. I said yes, and it turned out that the "something light and simple" was homemade ravioli and homemade watermelon juice. It was delicious.

When I woke up the next day, I went out to the patio to talk to the landlord, but I also met a neighbor there who spoke to me in Danish. Normally, I find it difficult to understand spoken Danish, but he spoke the most understandable Danish I've ever heard. Apparently, his mother was from Denmark.



A picture of my room. Desk and wardrobe.

Then I got a ride into town. My accommodation is a bit outside the city, surrounded by vineyards. It takes 10-15 minutes to take the bus to the city center, and the bus runs at least every 20 minutes. Actually, I can't really complain, but in Ume√•, I lived "closer" to everything. However, the University of Trento is spread out throughout the city. The department I will be studying at is also outside the city, closer to my accommodation, not within walking distance but definitely within cycling distance.



View of Trento.

Down in town, I had lunch and explored the city center. Trento has a relatively large center with picturesque old houses, pedestrian streets, and churches. Then I went to Lidl and bought groceries. It might not be very adventurous to buy the exact same pasta and crushed tomatoes that I buy at home in Sweden. But you don't always have to be adventurous. I will be here for half a year, so I have time.



Some old houses.

When I got home, it started raining heavily, and it continued to rain all night. It's supposed to be rainy for a few days now, but I don't mind. I prefer rain and 20 degrees over sun and 35 degrees.

Today, I realized that Trento barely has any sunrise or sunset since the city is situated in a valley surrounded by high mountains. I've never thought that you need a horizon to be able to see a sunset, but it's actually quite logical.

I'll take it easy in the upcoming days. I don't feel a strong urge to travel right now. I'll also try to get in touch with some other exchange students who have already arrived here. I've become a member of Trento University's association for Erasmus students, maybe I'll go downtown and pick up my membership card tomorrow.



View of the Alps from the flight to Italy.

Now I'm going to have pyttipanna for dinner.
